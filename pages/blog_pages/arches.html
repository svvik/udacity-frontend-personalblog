Arches are never enough!

Natural sandstone arches can stole your breath just by one glace on them. Such beauty deserve to be visited more than once. More than 2,000 stone arches can be found in Arches National Park.

Double Arch

Double Arch takes its name because of it consists of two arcs that share the same stone as a foundation for both of their outer legs. Interesting fact that Double Arch was formed differently from most of the arches in the park. This arch was formed by water erosion from above rather than more typical erosion from the side.

How to get there?

Double Arch is located in Arches National Park, Grand County, Utah, United States. Hike to Double Arch is easy and takes only 0.5 miles. Accessible for strollers and wheelchairs.

Is it free?

Timed Entry Ticket Fee: $2.00. But there is a Fee Free Days.

If you are planning to visit the Double Arch, you can find current information here: www.nps.gov

Delicate Arch

Among a lot of arches in Arches National Park there is one arch, which is world famous and called as Delicate Arch. You definitely saw this arch on Windows XP wallpaper. Delicate Arch is a 52-foot-tall freestanding natural arch (it the largest freestanding arch in the park) and was formed of Entrada Sandstone

How to get there?

Delicate Arch is located in Arches National Park, Grand County, Utah, United States. To see Delicate Arch up close and personal is a 3-mile round trip hike with 480 feet of elevation gain (will take 2-3 hours). Not such close look to the arch, just one mile away, can be observed at Lower Delicate Arch Viewpoint, take a short distance of walk. Nearby, the Upper Viewpoint (0.5 mi walk with stairs) offers a slightly less obstructed view.

Is it free?

Timed Entry Ticket Fee: $2.00. But there is a Fee Free Days.

If you are planning to visit the Delicate Arch, you can find current information here: www.nps.gov
