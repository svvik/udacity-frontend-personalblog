Deep Blue Lake

This unique place is a result of eruption of Mount Mazama.

After eruption of Mount Mazama and collapse of the summit of the volcano, the caldera filled with water to form Crater Lake. This lake famous for its beautiful blue color. Intresting fact that no rivers flow into or out of the lake (only water sources are snow and rain) and still it is the deepest lake in the United States, and the ninth deepest lake in the world. Crater Lake features two small islands: Wizard Island and Phantom Ship.

How to get there?

Crater Lake is located in Klamath County at Crater Lake National Park, Oregon, United States. You can access to Crater Lake from West Entrance, South Entrance or North Entrance. Check in advance state of the roads.

Is it free?

No reservations are needed to enter the park. Fees: private Vehicles - $30 in the summer (mid-May to October 31), $20 in the winter (November 1 to mid-May).

If you are planning to visit the Crater Lake, you can find current information here: www.nps.gov
