Hunting Waterfalls in Oregon

Waterfalls are without a doubt one of the most beautiful creations of nature. And Oregon is home to some of the must-sees among of them.

Multnomah Falls

Multnomah Falls ranks as the tallest waterfall in Oregon. It has a total height of 620 feet, with the upper falls being 542 feet and the lower segment being 69 feet. Even more mysterious look to the waterfall gives an Multnomah Creek Bridge, built in 1914.

How to get there?

Multnomah Falls is located on Multnomah Creek in the Columbia River Gorge, Oregon, United States. You can access it from the Interstate 84 parking lot at Exit 31.

Is it free?

Keep in mind that tickets are required to visit the Multnomah Falls Recreation Area and The Historic Lodge. Reservation cost $2 per ticket

If you are planning to visit the Multnomah Falls Recreation Area and The Historic Lodge, you can find current information here: www.fs.usda.gov

Trail of Ten Falls

More waterfalls could be found on Trail of Ten Falls. Yes, there are no less than ten beautiful falls on this a 7.2-mile loop trail: South Falls, Lower South Falls, Lower North Falls, Double Falls, Middle North Falls, Drake Falls, Winter Falls, Twin Falls, North Falls and Upper North Falls. The difficulty of this trail is moderate, but it definitely worth it.

How to get there?

The trail of Ten Falls is located in the northwest section of the Sillver Falls State Park, Oregon.

Is it free?

Fee for parking is $5 per vehicle

If you are planning to visit the Trail of Ten Falls, you can find current information here: https://stateparks.oregon.gov/

Toketee Falls

Toketee meaning "pretty" or "graceful" in Chinook Jargon. And honestly Toketee Falls are really one of the most graceful waterfalls in Oregon. Toketee Falls drops approximately 120 feet in two stages. The hike to the Toketee Falls viewing platform is short and easy.

How to get there?

Toketee Falls is a waterfall in Douglas County, Oregon, United States. From Roseburg, OR turn onto OR-138 E/NE Diamond Lake Boulevard. Turn north off Highway 138 at milepost 58.6 onto Forest Road 34 (Toketee-Rigdon Road). Cross the first bridge and turn left to the trailhead parking. Trailhead is at the far end of the parking area.

Is it free?

Parking and access to the falls are free

If you are planning to visit the Toketee Falls, you can find current information here: www.fs.usda.gov
